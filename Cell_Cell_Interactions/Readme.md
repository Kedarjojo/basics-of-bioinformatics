This study investigates how neutrophil–macrophage communication varies across three anatomical
compartments: bone marrow (BM), blood, and injured tissue. Using single-cell RNA sequencing data and the
NicheNet framework, I inferred ligand–target interactions from neutrophils to macrophages within each
compartment. By integrating cell-type-specific expression, ligand–receptor knowledge, and downstream
macrophage gene programs, we identified compartment-specific and conserved signaling axes. My analysis
reveals a shared core of ligands involved in macrophage maintenance and lipid handling, alongside distinct
compartment-specific signaling programs reflecting homeostasis, systemic inflammation, and tissue repair.
